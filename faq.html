Getting Started
Do I need prior coding experience?
No prior coding experience is required. The programs start from the fundamentals and gradually build toward practical skills.
What are the basic requirements?
Basic computer skills, a willingness to learn, and the ability to commit a few hours each week are enough to get started.
Schedule & Format
How many hours per week should I plan?
Plan for about 4–6 hours per week, including live sessions and practice time. Consistency matters more than speed.
Can I do this while working full-time?
Yes. The programs are designed for working professionals with weekend sessions and a manageable weekly commitment.
What happens if I fall behind?
Missing a session occasionally is manageable. We can help you catch up. The key is staying consistent each week.
Learning & Curriculum
How is this different from YouTube or self-paced online courses?

Most online courses provide information. Our focus is structured learning, guided practice, and real understanding.

At doors2ai you get:

  • A clear learning path instead of random topics
  • Live coaching sessions where you can ask questions
  • Practical projects to build real confidence
  • Guidance from industry professionals
  • A focused cohort environment to stay consistent

The goal is not just to watch tutorials, but to build real skills you can use.

Will I receive a certificate after completing the program?

Yes. You will receive a doors2ai certificate after completing the learning path and successfully finishing the required projects.

Certificates are awarded based on participation and project work to reflect the practical skills and understanding you build during the program.

Support & Outcomes
Do you provide career guidance or support?

We provide guidance on how to continue your learning journey, build practical projects, and strengthen your technical foundation.

We also share practical advice on portfolios, learning paths, and industry expectations to help you prepare for real-world opportunities.

What is the typical class size?
We typically keep cohorts between 5 and 8 learners (maximum 10) to maintain quality interaction, personal guidance, and time for questions.
